About TASC

The Theory of AI for Scientific Computing (TASC) workshop aims to bridge the gap between theoretical advancements in artificial intelligence and their practical applications in scientific computing. This workshop brings together researchers, practitioners, and theorists to explore innovative approaches, methodologies, and frameworks that leverage AI to solve complex scientific computing problems.

Join us for an exciting program featuring keynote presentations, paper sessions, and networking opportunities. We welcome contributions that advance our understanding of AI in scientific computing contexts and demonstrate novel applications across various scientific domains.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions on topics including, but not limited to:

  • Theoretical foundations of AI for scientific computing
  • Novel neural architectures for scientific problems
  • Physics-informed machine learning
  • AI for high-performance computing optimization
  • Interpretable and explainable AI for scientific applications
  • Uncertainty quantification in AI for science
  • Foundation models for scientific domains
  • Differentiable programming for scientific computing
  • AI for computational chemistry and materials science
  • Theoretical advances in neural solvers for PDEs

Submission Requirements:

Papers should be submitted as PDF files using the provided ACM template. Submissions must be limited to 8 pages, including figures and references. All papers will undergo a double-blind peer review process. Accepted papers will be presented at the workshop and published in the proceedings.

Review Criteria:

Submissions will be evaluated based on originality, technical soundness, clarity of presentation, and relevance to the workshop themes. We particularly encourage submissions that advance theoretical understanding while demonstrating practical impact.
